Create comprehensive roadmap - identify risks and opportunities

Execute

Process and implement with clear tasks and activities

Iterate

Outcome and results should be verified, refined and monitored. Optimize with new cycles

The Iron Triangle, also known as the Triple Constraint, is a fundamental concept in project management that highlights the three key elements crucial for any project's success:

  • Cost: The budget allocated for the project, covering all expenses related to resources, materials, and labor.
  • Time: The schedule for the project, including deadlines and milestones.
  • Scope: The specific goals, deliverables, and requirements of the project.

These elements are interconnected, meaning changes to one will inevitably impact the others. Balancing these constraints is essential for successful project management. Effective management ensures that the project stays within budget, meets deadlines.
